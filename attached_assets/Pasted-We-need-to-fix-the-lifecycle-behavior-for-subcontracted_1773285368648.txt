We need to fix the lifecycle behavior for subcontracted jobs after payment is completed.

Goal

When a subcontracted/referral job is fully paid and completed, the job should behave like a normal paid job in EcoLogic:

it should auto-delete / disappear from active job lists

but it should keep all invoice and payment history intact

So basically, I want subcontracted jobs to follow the same backend cleanup/archive behavior that regular paid jobs already use.

What is happening now

Right now, subcontracted jobs can still remain visible after they are paid/done, which is not what I want.

For normal jobs, once they are paid, the system already handles them correctly:

the active job goes away

invoice/payment records stay in history

I want the same behavior applied to subcontracted jobs.

What I need you to do
1) Trace how normal paid jobs are handled

First, find the existing source of truth for regular EcoLogic jobs after payment is completed.

I want you to trace:

what marks a normal job as fully paid

what backend logic removes / archives / hides it from active jobs

how invoice history is preserved

how payment records are preserved

Do not reinvent a new system if there is already an existing one for regular jobs.

2) Apply the same lifecycle logic to subcontracted jobs

Then update the subcontract/referral flow so that when a subcontracted job is:

paid in full

and considered completed/finalized according to the current business logic

it should no longer remain as an active job.

That means:

hide/remove/archive the subcontracted active job record the same way regular jobs are handled

preserve invoice records

preserve payment records

preserve any payout/referral audit history

do not break financial reporting/history

Important product behavior

I do not want financial records deleted.

Keep:

invoices

invoice line items

payment records

Stripe/payment references

payout/referral audit history

anything needed for history/reporting

Remove or hide from active workflow:

the active subcontracted job entry once it is fully paid/completed

So this is job lifecycle cleanup, not destructive financial deletion.

3) Match regular job backend behavior

Please use the same behavior/pattern as regular jobs wherever possible.

That means if normal jobs:

get archived instead of hard-deleted → do that

get filtered out of active job queries → do that

get marked completed/closed and hidden from active lists → do that

I want subcontracted jobs to be consistent with the rest of the app.

4) Handle both sides correctly

Please think through both company sides in a subcontract scenario:

Sending company

should keep invoice/payment/referral history

should not keep an active job lingering once fully paid/completed

Receiving company

should also behave correctly based on how the subcontracted job is represented on their side

preserve history, remove/hide active job as appropriate

Do not break the sender/receiver recordkeeping.

5) Trigger condition

Use the real existing logic for “paid and done.”

That might involve things like:

invoice fully paid

job marked complete

referral payout finalized

payment status succeeded

no remaining balance

Please trace the actual source of truth and use the same pattern regular jobs use.

Do not guess.

6) Verify affected queries/screens

After implementing, verify that fully paid subcontracted jobs no longer appear in places like:

active jobs lists

subcontractor-related active job sections

any contractor/referral active views where they should no longer show

But confirm that the history still exists in:

invoices

payments

any referral/payment audit records

7) Important constraints

do not delete invoice history

do not delete payment history

do not break Stripe/payment reconciliation

do not break subcontract payout logic

do not break referral activity history

do not change unrelated job behavior

do not introduce duplicate cleanup logic if regular jobs already have a pattern we can reuse

Debug / verification

Please add only targeted logs if needed and verify with a real subcontracted paid job.

I want confirmation of:

what exact condition triggered the cleanup/hide

what job record changed

what history records remained intact

Deliverables

Trace how regular paid jobs are currently handled

Apply the same paid/completed lifecycle behavior to subcontracted jobs

Remove/hide the active subcontracted job after it is paid/done

Preserve invoices, payments, and referral financial history

Verify it no longer shows in active job views but still exists in history

After implementation, tell me:

what existing regular-job logic you reused

whether subcontracted jobs are archived, soft-deleted, or filtered from active queries

what exact trigger marks them ready to be removed from active jobs

what financial/history records remain preserved

what screens/queries were verified after the fix
