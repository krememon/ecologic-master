We need to fix QuickBooks payment sync because Stripe payment succeeded, but QuickBooks still shows the invoice as unpaid.

BUG
A customer paid $1,000 successfully through Stripe, but in QuickBooks the invoice still shows:
- “Due in 30 days”
- “Receive payment”

So:
- Stripe payment succeeded
- QuickBooks invoice exists
- but QuickBooks is NOT marking the invoice paid

GOAL
When a Stripe payment succeeds for an invoice that is synced to QuickBooks, QuickBooks should also show that invoice as PAID by recording/applying the payment correctly.

IMPORTANT
Do NOT confuse:
- invoice sync
with
- payment sync

The invoice is clearly being created in QuickBooks already.
The bug is that the Stripe payment is not being reflected as a QuickBooks payment applied to that invoice.

WHAT TO INVESTIGATE

1) TRACE THE FULL FLOW FOR A STRIPE PAID INVOICE
For a normal invoice paid by Stripe, trace:
- Stripe payment success path
- EcoLogic payment record creation
- QuickBooks invoice sync lookup
- QuickBooks payment creation/apply step

2) IDENTIFY WHETHER PAYMENT SYNC EXISTS
Determine whether the current code:
- creates only the invoice in QuickBooks
- creates a QuickBooks payment object
- links/applies the payment to the QuickBooks invoice
- updates QuickBooks only for manual payments or not at all

3) FIND THE EXACT MISSING/BROKEN STEP
We need the exact broken link:
- no QuickBooks payment creation at all
- payment creation failed
- payment created but not applied to invoice
- wrong QuickBooks invoice id used
- payment sync only runs on certain payment methods
- Stripe success handler never triggers QuickBooks payment sync

4) VERIFY SOURCE OF TRUTH FIELDS
Check what IDs are stored for:
- EcoLogic invoice
- QuickBooks invoice id / doc number
- Stripe payment id / charge id
- QuickBooks customer id
- any QuickBooks payment record id

5) FIX QUICKBOOKS PAYMENT APPLICATION
After successful Stripe payment, ensure QuickBooks receives the correct payment sync:
- create/apply payment to the matching QuickBooks invoice
- mark the invoice paid in QuickBooks
- keep amount/customer/invoice mapping correct
- avoid duplicates if webhook/retry happens

6) MAKE IT IDEMPOTENT
Do not create duplicate QuickBooks payments on retries/webhooks.
Use a guard/check if the payment was already synced to QuickBooks.

7) ADD TARGETED LOGS
Add concise logs for:
- Stripe payment success
- QuickBooks sync started
- invoice id mapping found
- payment create/apply attempted
- success/failure response from QuickBooks
- duplicate guard triggered or not

Avoid noisy logs.

DELIVERABLES
Return:
1. exact files changed
2. whether QuickBooks invoice sync was already working
3. exact reason QuickBooks payment sync was not marking invoice paid
4. what was changed to create/apply the payment correctly
5. confirmation that Stripe-paid invoices now show as paid in QuickBooks

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Stripe payment succeeds
- matching QuickBooks invoice is updated with an applied payment
- QuickBooks no longer shows “Receive payment”
- invoice displays as paid in QuickBooks
- no duplicate QuickBooks payments are created
