Update job payment UI after successful payment (Paid pill + Pay button replacement)

CURRENT
- Jobs have a green “Pay” button when an invoice is unpaid
- After payment (Card / Cash / Check), the payment succeeds correctly
- User sees a success screen (“Payment Success” or “Already Paid”)
- HOWEVER:
  - Job card does NOT visually update to show paid status
  - “Pay” button still exists after payment
  - No clear paid indicator on the job card itself

GOAL
- After ANY successful payment (Card, Cash, or Check):
  - Job card shows a “Paid” pill badge (top-right of the card)
  - “Pay” button is replaced by a “Paid” pill
  - Paid state is persistent across refreshes
  - No duplicate payments possible
- UX should clearly indicate payment completion without extra clicks

RULES
- Jobs list remains the source of truth
- No auto-opening modals
- No toast notifications for success
- UI must react immediately after payment
- Must work identically for:
  - Stripe (card)
  - Cash
  - Check

IMPLEMENTATION STEPS (DO IN THIS ORDER)

1) ADD PAID STATE TO JOB DATA
- Ensure job or invoice has a reliable paid flag:
  - e.g. invoice.status === "paid"
- This state must persist in the DB
- Do NOT rely on local state only

2) UPDATE JOB CARD UI
- In JobCard component:
  - If invoice is unpaid:
    - Show green “Pay” button
  - If invoice is paid:
    - REMOVE “Pay” button entirely
    - SHOW a “Paid” pill badge instead
- Paid pill:
  - Green background
  - White checkmark icon (optional)
  - Text: “Paid”
  - Non-clickable

3) ADD PAID PILL TO TOP-RIGHT OF JOB CARD
- Display a small “Paid” pill in the job card header area
- This is visible in:
  - Jobs list
  - Client → Jobs tab
- Ensure spacing and alignment remain clean

4) UPDATE PAYMENT FLOWS TO TRIGGER UI REFRESH
- After successful payment (Card / Cash / Check):
  - Refetch job + invoice data
  - Clear any stale job state
  - Return user to Jobs list or Job view
- NEVER leave user on a success screen without updated job state

5) PREVENT DUPLICATE PAYMENTS
- If invoice is already paid:
  - Hide “Pay” button everywhere
  - Show “Already Paid” screen if user somehow reaches payment route
- No double charging, no double marking paid

ACCEPTANCE TESTS
- Pay with Card → Job card immediately shows “Paid” pill
- Pay with Cash → Job card immediately shows “Paid” pill
- Pay with Check → Job card immediately shows “Paid” pill
- Refresh page → Paid state persists
- No Pay button appears on paid jobs
- No regressions to Stripe, checkout, or invoice totals
