Next fix: on the sender-side invoice detail page for a referred/subcontracted job, the sender should not have the ability to Pay or Send Invoice.

What I mean

Using this current test case:

pjpell077 = sender

ppellegrino = subcontractor / receiver

the job was subcontracted out to ppellegrino

So once that happens, the sender-side invoice detail page should still show the financial information/history, but it should not show action buttons that imply the sender is the one collecting payment from the customer.

Right now on the sender-side invoice detail page, I still see:

Pay $2,000.00

Send Invoice

That is wrong for this referred job on the sender side.

Goal

For a referred/subcontracted invoice, the action buttons should respect which side is actually responsible for collecting payment.

Receiver / subcontractor side

The company that accepted the subcontracted job and is actually collecting payment should be the side that can:

Pay

Send Invoice

Sender side

The company that subcontracted the job out should not see those payment-collection buttons on that referred invoice detail page.

They should still be able to:

view the invoice

view the referred payment breakdown

view status/history

understand their share

But they should not be able to collect payment from that page.

Desired behavior
Sender-side referred invoice detail

Hide/remove:

Pay

Send Invoice

any other customer-collection action that only belongs to the receiver side

Receiver-side referred invoice detail

Keep:

the proper payment collection actions

the ability to collect/send invoice if that is the correct current workflow

Important clarification

This is specifically for referred/subcontracted invoices where the current viewer is the sender/originating company, not the company actually collecting the money.

I am not saying to remove Pay/Send Invoice from:

normal non-referred invoices

receiver-side referred invoices where payment collection is correct

What I need you to do
1) Detect sender-side vs receiver-side on referred invoices

Use the real source-of-truth relationship for the referred invoice:

who sent the referral

who received/accepted the referral

which company owns the collection side

which company is viewing the invoice page

Then determine whether the current viewer is:

sender
or

receiver

2) Hide actions for sender-side referred invoices

If the invoice is tied to a referred job and the current viewer is the sender:

do not render the Pay button

do not render the Send Invoice button

3) Preserve receiver-side behavior

If the current viewer is the receiver/collection side:

keep the relevant invoice/payment action buttons working normally

UI expectation

On the sender-side referred invoice page, the page should feel more like:

a financial/detail/history view

Not:

a payment collection screen

So it is fine if the sender still sees:

invoice number

amount

referred breakdown

status

linked job

customer info if appropriate

dates/history

But no payment collection actions.

Important constraints

do not break normal invoice detail pages

do not break receiver-side payment collection

do not break referred invoice history/detail rendering

do not change payment math

do not change invoice ownership rules unless needed for proper gating

do not remove actions globally; this should be conditional based on referred invoice role
